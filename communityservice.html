Available Donations

These are foods with a long shelf life that don't require refrigeration. Examples include canned goods (vegetables, fruits, soups), pasta, rice, cereal, beans, and shelf-stable pantry items.

Donations of fresh fruits and vegetables can provide essential nutrients. Local farms, grocery stores, and community gardens often contribute to fresh produce donations.

Items like milk, eggs, cheese, yogurt, and protein-rich foods such as canned meats (tuna, chicken), peanut butter, and beans are commonly donated.

In addition to food, some donation programs may accept hygiene products, toiletries, and non-food essentials that contribute to overall well-being.

Monetary contributions are often welcome as they allow organizations to purchase specific items, cover operational costs, and address any urgent needs.
